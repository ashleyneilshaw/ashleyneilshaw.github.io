About

I was recently awarded my PhD in Philosophy from the Philosophy Department at UCL in 2019, where I was also a Teaching Assistant.

I work primarily in the Philosophy of Mind and Action. I am interested in the nature of desire, rational agency, reasons, and how they are related. Recently, I have been working on the metaphysics of need, the role of teleology in social cognition, and self-knowledge of desire.

Research

Publications

  1. Desire and What it's Rational to Do | ±
    accepted at Australasian Journal of Philosophy
    2. It is often taken for granted that our desires can contribute to what it is rational for us to do. This paper examines an account of desire that promises an explanation of this datum, the guise of the good. I argue that extant guise-of-the-good accounts fail to provide an adequate explanation of how a class of desires—basic desire—contribute to practical rationality. I develop an alternative guise-of-the- good account on which basic desires attune us to our reasons for action in virtue of their biological function. This account emphasises the role of desire as part of our competence to recognise and respond to normative reasons.
  2. Desire and Satisfaction | ±
    2020. The Philosophical Quarterly, 70 (279): 371-384, doi: 10.1093/pq/pqz068.
    3. Desire satisfaction has not received detailed philosophical examination. Yet intuitive judgments about the satisfaction of desires have been used as data points guiding theories of desire, desire content and the semantics of 'desire'. This paper examines desire satisfaction and the standard propositional view of desire. First, I argue that there are several distinct concepts of satisfaction. Second, I argue that separating them defuses a difficulty for the standard view in accommodating desires that Derek Parfit described as "implicitly conditional on their own persistence", a problem posed by Shieva Kleinschmidt, Kris McDaniel and Ben Bradley. The solution undercuts a key motivation for rejecting the standard view in favour of more radical accounts proposed in the literature.

Other

  1. Paper on the Pragmatics of Slurs (joint work with Poppy Mankowitz) ±
    in progress.
    2. We provide a pragmatic explanation of the '(non-)projection' behaviour of slurring expressions.
  2. Paper on Appetite and Rational Agency ±
    in progress.
    3. I provide an account of how appetitive states like hunger, thirst or sexual desire provide agents with reasons for action. In doing so, I reconsider pervasive assumptions about both the nature of reason-responsive action.
  3. Paper on Affect and Practical Rationality ±
    under revise and resubmit.
    4. I argue that affective experiences like attraction and aversion do not provide reasons in virtue of their phenomenal character.
  4. Desire and Practical Rationality (PhD thesis) ±
    5. How do desires rationalise intentional action? I defend an account of desire that I call the Attunement View, a teleo-functional rehabilitation of the ancient thesis that desires present their objects as normatively favoured (the 'guise of the good'). This project has culminated in several articles: 'Desire and Satisfaction' is based on material from Ch.1, 'Desire and What it's Rational to Do' is based on Ch.4-6, and an article based on Ch.3 is under review.

Final year and masters-level course on natural language semantics including topics on variables and binding, quantification, counterfactuals, and demonstratives. Course text: Heim & Kratzer, Semantics in Generative Grammar. The course instructor was Ethan Nowak.
