About

Hi, I’m Ashley Shaw. I am a PhD candidate and Postgraduate Teaching Assistant in the UCL Philosophy Department. I started my PhD in 2014. My research is supported by the AHRC London Arts and Humanities Partnership. Before starting my PhD, I completed the MPhil Stud. programme at UCL.

From September 2017 - December 2017, I will be a Visiting Research Student at Arché Philosophical Research Center at the University of St. Andrews.

Research

Core Research

I work primarily in the Philosophy of Mind, Action and Philosophical Psychology. My Ph.D thesis concerns the role of desire in practical rationality. I develop a framework for understanding how our conative system comprises a capacity for non-deliberative responsiveness to a class of normative reasons for action. The account emphasises the biological functions of desire and the broader conative system in which they are embedded. I am interested more generally in the nature of desire, rational agency and acting for reasons.

Additional Interests

My research also seeks to connect these issues with topics in moral philosophy, linguistic semantics and epistemology. For instance, I am working on issues like the nature of pejorative and expressive language, and the semantics of desire ascriptions. I have also worked on self-knowledge with respect to conscious judgements and beliefs. In my M.Phil dissertation, I defended a ‘transparency’ account of self-knowledge. I hope to develop this work in the future to account for attitudes like desire.

Teaching
