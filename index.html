About

Hi, I’m Ashley Shaw. I am a PhD candidate and Postgraduate Teaching Assistant in the UCL Philosophy Department. I started the PhD in 2014. My research is supported by the AHRC London Arts and Humanities Partnership. Before starting my PhD, I completed the MPhil Stud. programme at UCL.

From September 2017 - December 2017, I will be a Visiting Research Student at Arché Philosophical Research Center at the University of St. Andrews.

Research

Core Research

I work primarily in the philosophy of mind and the philosophy of language. My PhD dissertation concerns the psychology and content of conative attitudes like desire, and the relationship between desire and practical rationality.

Additional Interests

My research also seeks to connect these issues with topics in metaethics, linguistic semantics and epistemology. For instance, I am working on issues like the nature of pejorative and expressive language, and the semantics of desire ascriptions. I have also worked on epistemology and self-knowledge with respect to conscious judgements and beliefs. In my MPhil dissertation, I defended a ‘transparency’ account of self-knowledge. I hope to develop this work in the future to account for attitudes like desire.

Teaching

As Postgraduate Teaching Assistant

PHILG098 Linguistic Semantics (Spring 2017) (Dr. Ethan Nowak, UCL) Final year and masters-level course on natural language semantics including topics on variables and binding, quantification, counterfactuals, and demonstratives. Course text: Heim & Kratzer, Semantics in Generative Grammar.
PHIL2045 Philosophy of Language (Autumn 2017) (Dr. Ethan Nowak, UCL) Second year module on a range of topics in the philosophy of language including: proper names, definite descriptions, modality, mental content, pragmatics, speech act theory.
PHIL1013 Introduction to Logic 2 (Spring 2015; 2016) (Dr. Luke Glynn, UCL) First year module introducing predicate logic. Course text: Barwise and Etchemendy, Language, Proof and Logic.
PHIL1014 Introduction to Logic 1 (Autumn 2015) (Dr. Luke Glynn, UCL) First year module introducing propositional logic. Course text: Barwise and Etchemendy, Language, Proof and Logic.
PHIL1012 Knowledge and Reality (Autumn 2014) (Dr. Rory Madden, UCL) First year introductory module on a range of topics in epistemology (analysis of knowledge; perception), metaphysics (metaphysics of objects; existence), philosophy of mind (physicalism and consciousness)
